## Notes on Curation

1. **Deduplication:** 1355 raw entries from 10 JSONL files were deduplicated to 1064 unique papers (by title, keeping highest relevance score).

2. **Selection criteria:**
   - All relevance-3 papers (185) were reviewed; ~100 most relevant are included above.
   - Relevance-2 papers (267) were reviewed for high importance; select few included.
   - Relevance-1 papers (612) were searched for foundational authors (Strogatz, Ott, Golubitsky, Motter, Palacios) and key concepts; a small number of essential foundational references included.

3. **Key themes that emerged:**
   - The Motter group (Zhang, Nishikawa, Motter) has produced the most substantial body of work on converse symmetry breaking and disorder-enhanced synchronization.
   - The Golubitsky-Stewart school provides the equivariant bifurcation theory foundation.
   - Palacios et al. (2024) bridges these by using normal forms and parameter symmetry breaking to rigorously characterize the barycentric condition.
   - Application domains include laser arrays, power grids, neural networks, and electronic oscillators.
   - Both theoretical (Ott-Antonsen, normal forms, master stability) and experimental (electrochemistry, lasers) approaches are represented.

4. **Papers NOT included despite relevance scores:**
   - Many relevance-2 and relevance-3 papers on tangentially related topics (quantum synchronization, specific biological models, purely computational GNN papers) were excluded to maintain focus on the core research question.
   - Application-specific papers (ADC calibration, specific robotics) were excluded unless they contained fundamental insights.
